Amidst an unprecedented shortage, the price of paper has jumped 7.6% since 2020

Low supply and increased demand have contributed to historic prices for paper mills and printing presses

By Julia Capizzi

November 12, 2021

When news of the book shortage broke earlier this fall, publishers, book retailers and supply-chain experts alike shared that an unprecedented paper shortage was partially to blame.

Now, it appears that paper prices have skyrocketed in the last year, according to a report from the Bureau of Labor Statistics Beta Labs.

John Beall is the East Region Sales Director for Sheridan, a printing press that manufactures books for major New York publishers like Simon & Schuster and Penguin Randomhouse. He shared that the paper shortage is partially to blame for the rising costs that his and other presses are facing.

“I wouldn’t say that the rise in paper costs has affected or contributed to paper shortages,” Beall said. “But that they are interconnected based on basic economics – where prices rise when supply is limited with high demand.”
